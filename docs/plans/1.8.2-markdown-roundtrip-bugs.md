# Plan 1.8.2: Markdown round-trip bugs — content loss and structural damage

## Summary

The document round-trip test (md → JSON → md) compares output to the original. **Acceptable** differences are minor: extra blank lines, extra spaces, or trivial formatting. **Unacceptable** differences are whole content missing, tables emptied, list items or headings stripped, or paragraphs broken.

This plan catalogues all known **unacceptable** round-trip bugs (content loss / structural damage). Diffs used in the test may only cover acceptable differences; see **Policy** below.

---

## Policy

- **Acceptable round-trip differences:** Extra or fewer blank lines, trailing/leading spaces, or whitespace-only line changes. These may be documented in a small `*-roundtrip-output.diff` (see tests/markdown/README.md).
- **Unacceptable:** Any loss of non-whitespace content, empty tables that should have cells, list items or headings that lose body text, or paragraphs split/lost. The test must **fail** when round-trip produces such differences; we do not accept large “fix-up” diffs that paper over content loss.
- **Enforcement:** The test script rejects any `*-roundtrip-output.diff` larger than a small line limit (e.g. 25 lines). Files that need a larger diff are treated as round-trip failures and must be fixed in the parser/document model (or excluded until fixed).

### Symptom summary (all above files)

1. **Paragraphs with bold/italic** — Text after an inline format (e.g. after “**read‑only”) is sometimes lost or split; paragraph becomes blank lines or truncated.
2. **Tables** — Cell content is dropped; round-trip emits header and separator but empty cells (or wrong structure).
3. **Ordered lists with bold at start** — “1. **Title**” and following paragraph/indented content: list item becomes “1. ” plus blank lines; body text lost.
4. **Headings with following block content** — Heading line can remain but the following table/list/paragraph block is lost or emptied.
5. **Unordered list items** — Bullet items can become “- ” with no content.

---

## Likely causes (to investigate)

- **Parser:** Block or inline handling when bold/italic appears in paragraphs or list items; table cell parsing or emission; list-item content boundary (e.g. continuation lines).
- **Document model:** How blocks (paragraph, list, table, heading) and inlines are stored and reassembled in `to_markdown()`.
- **Writer / Render:** Emission of tables, list items, and paragraph text; newline/blank handling between blocks.

No single root cause is confirmed; the above files mix paragraphs, tables, lists, and inline formatting in ways that currently break round-trip.
